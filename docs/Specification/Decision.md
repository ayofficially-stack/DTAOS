# DTAOS Decision Specification

> Define how architectural and operational decisions are recorded within DTAOS.

---

# Purpose

Decisions record approved architectural or operational choices.

A Decision exists to prevent the same discussion from happening repeatedly.

Once accepted, a Decision becomes part of the DTAOS operating system.

---

# Philosophy

Lessons define reusable operating rules.

Decisions define approved directions.

A Lesson answers:

"What should we do?"

A Decision answers:

"What have we decided?"

---

# Decision Template

Every Decision follows the same structure.

# Title

## Status

Accepted | Superseded | Deprecated

---

## Date

Decision approval date.

---

## Context

Why was this decision necessary?

---

## Decision

What has been approved?

---

## Rationale

Why was this option selected?

---

## Consequences

Positive outcomes

Negative outcomes

---

## Alternatives Considered

What other options were evaluated?

Why were they rejected?

---

## Related

Related Projects

Related Lessons

Related Technologies

---

## Rule

One clear statement that becomes the operating rule.

---

# Writing Principle

A Decision records an approved direction.

Do not record discussions.

Do not record temporary ideas.

A Decision should remain valid until explicitly superseded.

---

# Quality Standard

A Decision should answer:

- What was decided?
- Why was it decided?
- What alternatives were rejected?
- What rule should future projects follow?

---

# Goal

Every major architectural or operational decision should be documented once.

The same decision should never need to be made twice.

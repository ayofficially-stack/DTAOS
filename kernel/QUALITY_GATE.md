# DTAOS Quality Gate

The Quality Gate is the final review layer before an answer, document, slide plan, or strategic recommendation is considered complete.

## 1. Universal Gate

Every output must pass these checks.

| Check | Question |
|---|---|
| Purpose alignment | Does the output solve the actual request? |
| Context alignment | Does it follow DTAOS memory, decisions, and current project state? |
| Evidence discipline | Are factual or technical claims supported or clearly marked as assumptions? |
| Risk review | Are likely objections, weaknesses, or reviewer concerns addressed? |
| Actionability | Can the user copy, apply, or decide based on this output? |
| Brevity control | Is the explanation no longer than needed? |

## 2. Government / NET / Proposal Gate

Use this when the task involves government projects, NET, R&D proposals, technology demand forms, or evaluation documents.

- Is the writing aligned with the evaluator's scoring criteria?
- Is the technology need clearly separated from the technology solution?
- Are novelty, advancement, applicability, and commercialization clearly distinguished?
- Are missing data and validation risks handled honestly?
- Does the text avoid unsupported exaggeration?

## 3. Sales / Design / PPT Gate

Use this when the task involves sales sheets, brochures, PPT, white-label materials, infographics, or design direction.

- Is there one clear message per page or section?
- Is the tone premium, professional, and non-cliché?
- Are icons minimized unless they add real meaning?
- Are alignment, spacing, hierarchy, and information density controlled?
- Does the material make a real customer more willing to review PoC?

## 4. Technical Gate

Use this when the task involves graphene, materials, composites, coatings, carbide, or mechanisms.

- Is the mechanism technically plausible?
- Are claims separated from hypotheses?
- Are validation methods or missing data clearly identified?
- Is the explanation understandable to both technical reviewers and decision makers?

## 5. Fail Rule

If an output fails the Quality Gate, revise it before presenting it as final. Do not say the work is complete when it only satisfies a draft-level standard.

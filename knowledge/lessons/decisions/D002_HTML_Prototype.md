# D002 — HTML as Editorial Prototype

## Status

Accepted

---

## Date

2026-07-02

---

## Context

DTAOS requires a fast and flexible method for validating layout, information hierarchy, and editorial direction before investing time in detailed PowerPoint editing.

During the P001 GrapheneAll White-label Sales Sheet project, HTML proved to be an efficient medium for rapid iteration and collaborative review.

---

## Decision

HTML shall be used as an editorial prototype.

HTML is not the final deliverable.

The purpose of HTML is to validate structure, layout, and design direction before producing the editable PowerPoint.

---

## Rationale

HTML provides:

- Fast iteration
- Immediate visual feedback
- Easy collaboration
- Low-cost revisions
- Efficient editorial review

Using HTML before PowerPoint significantly reduces redesign effort.

---

## Consequences

### Positive

- Rapid prototype generation
- Faster review cycles
- Reduced PowerPoint rework
- Better communication of design direction

### Negative

- Requires conversion to PowerPoint
- Prototype is not directly deliverable to customers

---

## Alternatives Considered

### PowerPoint as the First Draft

Rejected.

Reason:

Early-stage revisions in PowerPoint are slower and increase editorial cost.

---

### AI-generated Images as Prototype

Rejected.

Reason:

Images are difficult to edit and unsuitable for collaborative refinement.

## Rule

Prototype First.

Deliverable Second.

HTML is used to validate direction.

Editable PowerPoint is used to produce the final deliverable.
